My first interview in this field

Back in 2006, a friend gave me a computer which did not function very well. It was old, slow, and had multiple viruses. I spent days trying to clean it up until I finally gave up and decided to buy a new one. Well a new PC in 2006 was very expensive since I worked at a fast food restaraunt and did not make much. My next option was to build one. Without any knowledge of what a CPU or a mother board was I took on the challenge. I did a lot of research and in two weeks I managed to build a functioning PC.

This led me to see what else I can do. So I created a resume and started applying for Service Desk positions in the area. About two weeks later IBM in Delaware contacted me for an interview. I remember taking my built PC to the interview and the interviewer laughing and asked "Why did I bring my home PC to an interview." I replied "May I do a ten minute demonstraton", the interviewer agreed, and I proceeded to take the PC apart and talked about what each component was, and it's functions. It took a day for the interviewer to make a decision and get back to me.

Shortly after I accepted the Service Desk position, I enrolled myself in a Bachelor's degree program and my career in the IT field took off. I have been working in the IT field mainly in the software profession for about 10 years now, and have worked in a number of computer programming languages and operating systems.
